]]> Wait, what’s this new Hackagong requirement?

Teams competing for the major ‚Äúmost viable business‚Äù prize are required to create a small crowdfunding campaign for their project. This does not apply to any of the category prizes like 3D printing, IoT, design, code etc.

  • Made Top 5? Put together a 3 minute pitch

    • Explain the problem you‚Äôre solving
    • Show what you built to solve it
    • How will it make money?
    • Share customer feedback
    • Share crowdfunding results
  • 3-5 minutes questions from judges

  • Public encouraged to back all of the running crowdfunding campaigns

  • You‚Äôre only 12 days in, so keep pushing your crowdfunding campaign
  • Work with your backers to refine and build out the product
  • Release, keep backers happy, change the world

What if we can’t make it to DemoDay?

Yeah it‚Äôs on a Friday to align with the MindShift event. If you‚Äôre in the Top 5 teams and can‚Äôt pitch on the day, you can either submit a video pitch which we will play on stage or we can Skype you in. Contact info@hackagong.com for any issues or concerns.

What is crowdfunding?

Crowdfunding is an awesome way to raise funds from a large group of people for a new project. KickStarter is the most well-known platform, famous for funding products such as the Pebble watch. However crowdfunding isn‚Äôt limited to physical consumer products. You can crowdfund anything. Many predict it will be the future of venture capital and startup financing.

Why are you being a meanie?

Hackagong likes to try new things. It‚Äôs the reason why we‚Äôve grown to become Australia‚Äôs largest hackathon in just 4 years. Our mission is to spark new innovations and maker communities, thus encouraging the formation of new startups in the Illawarra and Australia. The crowdfunding campaign requirement presents the following benefits:

  1. Competitors rapidly learn invaluable sales and marketing skills.
  2. Crowdfunding is the ultimate validation of product-market fit. If people give you money, they really want the product.
  3. Crowdfunding could potentially fund dozens of Hackagong team projects.
  4. Encourages teams to continue working on their projects after Hackagong.
  5. Provides an instant customer base and evangelised community.
  6. Judging for the overall prize becomes much more efficient.

What platform should I use?
It‚Äôs your choice. There are 100s of platforms in the wild that cater to different niches and have varied funding models. Something like Indiegogo is likely a better choice than KickStarter for this scenario. Wikipedia has a good comparison of platforms: http://wki.pe/Comparison_of_crowdfunding_services

How much should we raise?

It‚Äôs entirely up to you. We‚Äôre not expecting million dollar crowdfunding campaigns. If you raise $1000, that‚Äôs pretty awesome right? It‚Äôs also up to you whether you choose to use a platform that returns money to backers if the funding goal is not reached (‚ÄúAll or Nothing‚Äù), or a platform where all funds are kept (‚ÄúKeep it All‚Äù).

What perks shall I offer our backers?

Have a look around at what perks other small crowdfunding campaigns offer their backers. You likely don‚Äôt want to be printing tshirts or anything complex. At the least you could simply offer backers beta access to the product, or a subscription, or some other small perk. Your backers are likely to be friends/family wanting to support you, and others who actually see value in the product to solve their problems. Cater perks to both.

What about the campaign video?

Videos are very important to a crowdfunding campaign. That doesn‚Äôt mean you need an expensive, high-production value video. Use your phone, add some basic editing, make it short, funny, interesting and clearly explain the pain-point you‚Äôre solving for backers and how the product solves that pain-point. If your video is less than 3min, is compelling enough to collect a new backer and a social share‚Ä¶ perfect.

How to hustle and promote?

Learn fast, fail often. Brainstorm a list of things you could do, places to promote, people to contact, networks to leverage. Try them all. Test and measure. Whatever works best, keep doing that thing. There are also tonnes of resources online regarding tactics to promote crowdfunding campaigns.

How long should we run the campaign?

Usually you can set the timeframe yourself. Most campaigns run for 30 days. Since there are only 12 days between Hackagong and DemoDay, it makes sense to keep the campaign running for another ~18 days after DemoDay. We will promote all active campaigns at DemoDay and on all of our social channels.

What if we don’t meet our funding goal?

If you set a reasonable funding goal and did the best you can to promote, perhaps it‚Äôs just validation that the market demand/want for your product wasn‚Äôt really there. Refund backers, get feedback from them and reflect on the learnings for the next campaign.

What if we don’t want to continue on the project?

No worries, just ensure your backers are kept in the loop and nobody is burned. Refund backers, keep them happy. Those very backers are now potential evangelists and customers for your next project.

What if we raise so much money that we can ditch our job/uni and go change the world?

Awesome epicness. To the moon!
